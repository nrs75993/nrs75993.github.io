Liverpool Study Abroad

UGA Liverpool Summer Study Abroad 2020

Who Can Apply?

This study abroad program is available to UGA students enrolled in the Franklin College of Arts and Sciences. Students in other majors may apply, however, credit is given for a Sociology and Political Science course. Therefore, sociology, political science, and criminal justice majors typically apply to this program. UGA Franklin College of Arts & Science and SPIA host this program.

Even if you are not enrolled in these colleges, you may still apply. Regardless of your major, this trip is a wonderful opportunity to go abroad and experience another culture. For more information, click here

What are the Classes?

  1. SOCI4590 - Crime in Global Context

    This class is instructed by Professor Jody Clay-Warner. It is a three-hour course that focuses on national variation in definitions, incidence, and prevalence of crime; the role of culture, structure, and globalization on crime and crime control. This class meets Monday-Thursday during the duration of the program.

  2. POLS4781 - Criminal Justice Systems in the U.S. & U.K.

    This class is instructed by Professor Susan Haire. It is also a three-hour course that uses a comparative approach, drawing on the United States experience, to develop studentsâ€™ understanding of the British criminal justice system. This class meets Monday-Thursday during the duration of the program.
